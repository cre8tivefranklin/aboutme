Hello World

There's a lot to be said. For starters, I'm an NJ native; born and raised. My Hometown is Paterson. Anyway, I worked in NJ and then worked in the Greater New York area for a number of years. First, I started off working as a sales rep. In NJ, I spent 4 years at Sears before it tanked. Then I went on to work in Manhattan and spent a lot of time pitching over the phone. Was it fun? Not really. In 2017, I decided to make a major career change. I didn't have a clear vision of what I wanted that change to be. That said, I started learning how to code. I sucked at math, but I sank my teeth into it regardless. Meanwhile, I got absorbed by Manhattan's restaurant industry. In that time, I rose to the position of Sous Chef at an eatery chain called DIG(originally known as DIG INN). It was a fast-growing company, and my rank among their Chefs was also fast-growing. That is, until Covid. Things got ugly quickly. Fast forward a few years later(now), and here I am; a STEM student at PCCC in Paterson, NJ. I'm an Engineering Science major with aspirations of becoming a Biomedical Engineer. I started off as a simple hobbyist coder and decided to go full-time as a student. I've always been a huge science fiction geek. Specifically, the cyberpunk subgenre has always been a favorite category of mine. I love seeing and reading about the different visualizations of the line between man and technology being crossed. Designing and engineering prosthetics and implementing robotics is exciting to me.
